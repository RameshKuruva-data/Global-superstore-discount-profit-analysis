## Business Problem
The company observed strong revenue growth but declining profitability.
Management suspected that aggressive discounting might be eroding profit margins.
The goal of this analysis is to identify which sub-categories and discount levels
are causing losses and where discounts can be optimized without hurting sales.
## Dataset
- Source: Global Superstore dataset
- Rows represent individual product line items within an order
- Key fields include Sales, Profit, Discount, Sub-Category, Region, and Order Date
## Tools Used
- Excel: Initial data cleaning
- MySQL: Business analysis and aggregation
- Power BI: Dashboard and visualization
## Key Questions Answered
- How is the overall business performing in terms of revenue, profit, and margin?
- Does higher discounting reduce profit margins?
- Which sub-categories are most sensitive to discounts?
- Where should discounts be reduced to prevent losses?
## Key Insights
- Medium and high discounts significantly reduce profit margins for certain sub-categories.
- The Tables sub-category generates high sales but incurs losses at medium discount levels.
- Some sub-categories remain profitable even with moderate discounts, indicating selective discounting opportunities.
## Recommendations
- Reduce medium and high discount levels for discount-sensitive sub-categories like Tables.
- Maintain low discounts for sub-categories that remain profitable.
- Review shipping costs for low-margin products to improve profitability.
